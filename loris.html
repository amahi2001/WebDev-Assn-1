Slow Loris

The slow loris (Nycticebus coucang) is a strepsirrhine primate endemic to the forests of South and Southeast Asia. It is the only extant species in the genus Nycticebus. The slow loris is a nocturnal, arboreal animal, and is the only venomous primate. It has a round head, large eyes, and a snout-like protrusion. Its fur is typically brown or grey, and it has a long, prehensile tail. The slow loris is a solitary animal, and is active at night. It feeds on insects, small vertebrates, and fruit. It is a popular pet in Southeast Asia, and is often kept in a cage with a small tree. The slow loris is threatened by habitat loss and hunting for the pet trade. It is listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List.
