Tapir

The tapir is a large, herbivorous mammal with a short, prehensile snout. It is the only extant member of the family Tapiridae, and is divided into two extant species and two extinct species. Tapirs are native to South America and Southeast Asia. The two extant species are the Baird's tapir (Tapirus bairdii) and the Malayan tapir (Tapirus indicus). The extinct species are the South American tapir (Tapirus terrestris) and the Javan tapir (Tapirus javanicus).
